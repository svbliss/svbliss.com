SARAH-VIOLET BLISS is a filmmaker from Brooklyn, NY who has written and directed a number of award-winning films including features, shorts and webisodes.

Sarah-Violet's first feature film Fort Tilden, co-written and co-directed with Charles Rogers, has gathered a handful of awards including the Grand Jury Prize for Narrative Feature at the 2014 SXSW Film Festival and the Special Jury Prize for Narrative Feature at IFFBoston 2014. She also wrote and directed the segment Still Life from the multi-director feature film Tar which stars James Franco and premiered at the 7th Rome Film Festival.

Her short film Three, two premiered at The 40th Telluride Film Festival and screened at New Directors/New Films while her short Priceless Things has won numerous awards and can be seen on the second season of the PBS program Film School Shorts.

She also directed the episode Stevie of the highly acclaimed web series High Maintenance which is available to watch on Vimeo.

She is currently living and working in Los Angeles while touring Fort Tilden on the festival circuit.
